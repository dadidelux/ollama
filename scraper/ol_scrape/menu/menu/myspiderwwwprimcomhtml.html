ARB in a joint venture had EPC responsibility for a five-unit, GE LMS100-PA, natural gas fueled, air-cooled, Simple Cycle Combustion Turbine, with catalytic emission controls. El Segundo, CA

El Segundo Energy Center

BUSINESS UNIT
ARB Industrial
CAPABILITY
Energy/Renewables
PDF
Project Sheet

Replacement of the existing El Segundo Generating Station (ESGS) Units 1 and 2 with a natural gas-fired combined cycle electric generation facility. The new combined cycle is designed to generate 550 megawatts (MW) under nominal conditions. The project used Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR), a dry, low NOx combustor and an oxidation catalyst system to reduce air emissions. Construction of concrete foundations, electrical duct banks, all piping systems, two Siemens gas turbines each with a single pressure HRSGs and two Siemens steam turbines with air cooled condensers. Four GSUs, auxiliary transformers, electrical rooms, gas compressors, and other required Balance of Plant (BOP) support equipment.

Salt River Project

Project Sheet

The Salt River Project (SRP) Near Term Capacity Projects (NTC) each rated at 99 MW at two of its existing generating facilities. The two existing sites are Agua Fria and Desert Basin. Each NTC Site features two General Electric LM6000PC Units complete with an Inlet Air Filter, Evaporative Cooler and SCR/CO catalyst for emissions control (includes duct work, ammonia vaporization skid, tempering air fans and exhaust ducts), two 100% Fuel Gas Compressors (Agua Fria only), one natural gas conditioning skid, a three winding Generator step-up transformer, one Power Control Module, one Balance of Plant (BOP) Power Distribution Center (PDC), UAT’s and associated BOP equipment. ARB is performing the EPC construction of both facilities.

The project is comprised of two simple-cycle generating facilities consisting of two General Electric (GE) LM6000 hybrid enhanced gas turbine (Hybrid EGT®) systems.
#Energy
Stanton, CA

Stanton Energy Reliability Center (SERC) - 98MW

BUSINESS UNIT
ARB Industrial
CAPABILITY
Energy/Renewables
PDF
Project Sheet

The project is comprised of two simple-cycle generating facilities consisting of two General Electric (GE) LM6000 hybrid enhanced gas turbine (Hybrid EGT®) systems. The Hybrid EGT® combines a combustion gas turbine with an integrated battery storage component operated by a proprietary software system. The integrated system will be capable of providing synchronous condensing, greenhouse gas-free spinning reserve, high-speed regulation, primary frequency response, and voltage support with the combined response of the gas turbine and battery storage system. This design supports the move toward greater renewable energy in the CAISO energy market.

Divide Station
Divide Station is a regulator station where L-1010 and L 36-1007 meet and is located within the Santa Maria City limits.
#Utilities
Santa Maria, CA

Divide Station

BUSINESS UNIT
ARB Underground
CAPABILITY
Utilities
PDF
Project Sheet

Divide Station is a regulator station where L-1010 and L 36-1007 meet and is located within the Santa Maria City limits. This complete station re-build project is designed to upgrade Divide Station by replacing four regulators with four new control valves, adding a vessel to address issues with liquids in the pipeline, replacing the Tele-safe with a new SCADA Pak, installing a new five-hundred-foot deep well system with rectifier for cathodic protection, & relocating and upgrading the electric meter from 50 amps to 100 amps. A new downstream block valve was installed for isolation, as is commonplace when working within a station. All excavating was done by hand.

Line 2000 is a 30-inch high-pressure natural gas pipeline operating in the Southeast Desert Region.
#Utilities
Riverside County, CA

Line 2000 D Badlands

BUSINESS UNIT
ARB Underground
CAPABILITY
Utilities
PDF
Project Sheet

Line 2000 is a 30-inch high-pressure natural gas pipeline operating in the Southeast Desert Region. This hydro test consists of 7.76 miles of 30-inch HP pipeline, and two 30-inch MLVs as defined by So Cal Gas. This PSEP project included 12 separate test break locations. L-2000-D Badlands hydrotest project begins at the Beaumont Station and runs west through the Badlands terminating within the Moreno Valley Pressure Limiting Station. As part of this project, ARB Team members were tasked with re-building access roads throughout the Badlands territory as well as building a complex engineered platform and cribbing system to be able to perform all necessary work at test break 21 located on a 102-feet span in the Badlands mountain area. ARBU performed all strength testing & provided all testing documentation for this project.
